# 2026 MENTOR Alignment Meeting - Team Prep

**Meeting Purpose:** Align with MENTOR on 2026 strategy, priorities, and budget for Mentor Connector and NQMS systems.

**Prep Date:** January 14, 2026
**Client Meeting:** TBD

---

## Executive Summary

MENTOR's internal team met on 1/8/2026 to discuss their system strategy. Key insight: They're at a strategic crossroads about the **future vision** of Mentor Connector - should it remain a directory/discovery tool, or evolve into a **full CRM** for program management?

**Our Focus:** Help them articulate problems, prioritize solutions, and understand budget constraints before proposing technical approaches.

---

## Key Problems MENTOR is Trying to Solve

### 1. **Strategic Identity Crisis: Directory vs. CRM**
**The Problem:**
- Current system is a national directory/discovery tool for mentoring programs
- Affiliates and programs are requesting CRM functionality (track mentors/mentees, scheduling, communications)
- Team is divided on whether to build CRM features into MC or integrate with existing CRM systems
- High demand from field: "What's the best tool for tracking mentors and mentees?"

**Questions for MENTOR:**
- What is your 2026 vision: Discovery tool, CRM, or hybrid?
- Have you evaluated existing CRM solutions vs. building into MC?
- What specific CRM features do affiliates need most urgently?
- Is there budget for a discovery phase to assess CRM requirements?

---

### 2. **Data Quality and System Reliability**
**The Problem:**
- Duplicate entries in database (cleanup needed)
- Outdated program information due to high turnover
- No automated accountability for profile updates
- Programs go inactive but remain in public directory

**Current Solutions Implemented:**
- Duplicate name flagging (added 2025)
- Removed organization field to reduce confusion (2023 relaunch)

**Questions for MENTOR:**
- What level of data cleanup is budgeted for 2026?
- Priority: Automated profile pausing for inactive programs?
- Do they want automated reminders/AI-driven engagement?

---

### 3. **System Integration and API Development**
**The Problem:**
- **No APIs currently exist** - limits integration capabilities
- Cannot integrate with other systems (CRM, volunteer platforms, etc.)
- Past partnerships (Idealist, Volunteer Match, LinkedIn, NBA Foundation) underutilized
- Team wants to leverage data strategically but lacks technical infrastructure

**Questions for MENTOR:**
- Is API development a 2026 priority?
- Which integrations would provide the most value?
- Budget allocated for API infrastructure?

---

### 4. **User Account Management**
**The Problem:**
- **Cannot delete user accounts** - clutters database
- High turnover in programs = repeated questions and support needs
- NQMS recently added archiving feature; MC needs similar functionality

**Questions for MENTOR:**
- Priority level for user deletion/archiving feature?
- Do they want this aligned between MC and NQMS?

---

### 5. **Training and Onboarding**
**The Problem:**
- No standardized training (ad-hoc live sessions only)
- Resources exist on website but are overlooked
- High turnover = constant retraining needed
- Need for Learning Management System (LMS)

**Questions for MENTOR:**
- Is LMS development in scope for 2026?
- Budget for training materials development?
- Can they define onboarding requirements for new affiliates?

---

### 6. **Public-Facing Search Improvements**
**The Problem:**
- Tool promoted for "families to find mentoring" but focuses on recruiting adult volunteers
- Mentees are directed to contact programs (workaround, not direct feature)
- Programs concerned about waitlists and capacity
- Peak usage Aug-Oct (back to school) - opportunity for targeted improvements

**Questions for MENTOR:**
- Make public site more mentee-inclusive in 2026?
- Address waitlist/capacity management features?
- Improve search functionality - specific pain points?

---

### 7. **MC + NQMS Strategic Alignment**
**The Problem:**
- Two systems with overlapping needs but separate development
- NQMS has archiving; MC doesn't
- Need unified tech approach and governance

**Questions for MENTOR:**
- Committee formation timeline (mentioned in minutes)?
- Unified roadmap for both systems?
- Shared vs. separate budget for MC and NQMS?

---

## Budget Discovery Questions (CRITICAL)

**Primary Budget Questions:**
1. **What is the total budget allocated for MC/NQMS development in 2026?**
2. **Is budget divided by system (MC vs. NQMS) or pooled?**
3. **Are there separate buckets for:**
   - Data cleanup/maintenance
   - New feature development
   - API/infrastructure work
   - Training/LMS development
   - Discovery/planning phases
4. **Funding sources:** Grant-funded? Internal budget? Multi-year commitment?
5. **Budget approval timeline:** When will they know final numbers?

**Secondary Budget Considerations:**
- Maintenance retainer vs. project-based work preferences
- Appetite for phased development vs. all-at-once features
- Priority if budget is constrained: What gets cut first?

## Recommended Meeting Structure

### Phase 1: Problem Discovery (30 min)
- What are the top 3 problems keeping you up at night?
- What does success look like in December 2026?
- Who are your primary users and what are they struggling with?

### Phase 2: Prioritization Exercise (20 min)
- Walk through Tier 1/2/3 framework above
- Force-rank initiatives
- Identify dependencies

### Phase 3: Budget Discussion (15 min)
- Budget availability and timeline
- Funding constraints
- Approval process

### Phase 4: Next Steps (10 min)
- Determine if discovery phase is needed
- Set follow-up meeting schedule
- Define deliverables timeline

## Red Flags to Watch For

1. **Lack of internal alignment** - They're still debating CRM vs. directory vision
2. **No budget clarity** - May need to schedule follow-up after budget approval
3. **Scope creep risk** - Many ideas, unclear priorities
4. **Committee formation** - Could slow decision-making if not managed
5. **Expectation mismatch** - They may expect more for less given budget constraints

---

## Success Criteria for This Meeting

✅ **We leave with:**
- Clear understanding of top 3 priorities for 2026
- Budget range or timeline for budget approval
- Agreement on next steps (discovery phase, SOW, follow-up meeting)
- Documented priorities we can use for scoping

✅ **We avoid:**
- Proposing solutions before understanding problems
- Committing to timelines without budget clarity
- Technical implementation discussions before strategic alignment
- Over-promising on scope
